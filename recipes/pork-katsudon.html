Pork Katsudon

Description

A well-rounded meal, made from a combination of delicious ingredients such as meat, rice and vegetables.

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups cooked rice
  • 2 boneless pork chops (with a meat mallet)
  • Salt and Pepper
  • Flour, for dusting
  • 1 egg, beaten, for breading
  • 1 cup panko crumbs
  • ½ cup dashi stock (alternatively, chicken or vegetable stock)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons mirin
  • 2 teaspoons sugar
  • 1 small onion, sliced thin
  • 2 eggs
  • ¼ cup cooked peas

Instructions

  1. Heat up some oil in a deep pan - about ½ an inch should do.
  2. Pound the pork chops with the mallet to about ¼ of an inch thickness.
  3. Season the pork chops well with salt and pepper, and dust them with flour.
  4. Dip the pork first in the beaten egg, and then coat with the panko crumbs
  5. Once the oil is hot, place the breaded pork in and fry until golden brown on both sides. Once cooked through, set them aside on a paper towel (to absorb the oil).
  6. Add the stock, soy sauce, mirin and sugar to a pan (big enough to hold the pork) and bring to a simmer.
  7. Add in the onions and cover the pan with a lid.
  8. Once the onions are translucent, carefully slice the pork katsu and add them on top of the onions.
  9. Beat the remaining two eggs and pour them on top of the pork katsu slices. Cover the pan with the lid
  10. Start preparing the bowl by adding the rice
  11. Once the eggs are cooked to your liking, pick up the pan and slide/pour the sauce, onions, egg and katsu on top of the rice in the bowl.
  12. Top with peas and/or another choice of garnishing and enjoy!
